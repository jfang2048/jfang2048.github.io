Step 1: Case Study & Data Preparation

Germany was selected as the case study area. First, we obtained the national boundary from the vector layer containing boundary geometries and group attributes by selecting our group number, 1, in the Group attribute field. The workflow combines four main data sources:

  • CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10
  • ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover for 2021 and 2023
  • WorldPop 2023 Population Counts
  • FAO GAUL Level 2 Administrative Boundaries for Germany

Step 2: Pollutant Raster Processing

CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis data were processed in QGIS for NO₂, PM₂.₅, and PM₁₀. The December 2023 NetCDF files were loaded as mesh layers, aggregated into monthly mean rasters, and exported as GeoTIFF layers.

The December 2023 maps were combined with the monthly pollutant rasters for 2021 and 2023. After clipping all rasters to the Germany boundary, annual average maps were calculated with the GRASS r.series tool. These annual rasters became the base layers for pollutant classification and change detection.

Step 3: Pollutant Classification & AMAC Mapping

The 2023 annual average maps were reclassified into five concentration classes according to the lab threshold tables, with concentration values expressed in µg/m³. The classification was applied separately for NO₂, PM₂.₅, and PM₁₀ so that each pollutant could be compared with land cover and population data using the same five-level structure.

Annual Mean Aggregation Change (AMAC) maps were then calculated with pollutant_2023 - pollutant_2021. Negative AMAC values indicate areas where concentration decreased between 2021 and 2023, while positive values identify local increases.

Step 4: Land Cover Change Analysis

ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover rasters for 2021 and 2023 were clipped to Germany and combined into a transition raster using ("LC_2021" × 100) + "LC_2023". This encoding records both the original class and the 2023 class for each valid pixel. Area statistics were calculated in EPSG:3035 to use an equal-area reference system.

The assigned pollutant-land cover pairs were NO₂ with Built Area, PM₂.₅ with Crops, and PM₁₀ with Trees. For each assigned class, pixels were grouped as Stable, Gain, or Loss. These masks were used to summarize AMAC values by land cover transition, linking pollutant change with the main land cover dynamics.

Step 5: Population Exposure & Bivariate Mapping

WorldPop 2023 Population Counts were prepared for the Germany boundary and reclassified into five population classes. NoData values inside the study area were handled before classification so that empty cells and valid population cells could be interpreted consistently.

FAO GAUL Level 2 administrative boundaries were used as the reporting units. For each district, zonal statistics provided the median population class and the maximum pollutant concentration class. These values were combined with bivariate = pol_class_max × 10 + pop_class_median, creating a 5×5 pollutant-population exposure matrix.

Step 6: Output Integration & WebGIS Publication

The processed outputs were organized for communication through the Results and WebGIS pages. The Results page summarizes annual pollutant averages, concentration classes, AMAC maps, land cover transition statistics, zonal statistics, bivariate exposure layers, and population exposure charts.

The WebGIS page provides an interactive view of the main spatial outputs. It is designed to support map exploration with pollutant layers, land cover change layers, bivariate exposure layers, legends, and map controls, allowing the workflow results to be inspected directly in the browser.
