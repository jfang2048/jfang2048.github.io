Mapping Air Quality in Germany

Land cover transitions, pollutant change, and population exposure, 2021–2023

From GIS processing to WebGIS results

Germany case study using CAMS air quality reanalysis, ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover, WorldPop population, and FAO GAUL Level 2 administrative boundaries

All datasets were reprojected to WGS84 (EPSG:4326), clipped to Germany, classified, summarised with zonal statistics, and prepared for web publication. The WebGIS supports map interpretation through multiple basemaps, map controls, pollutant layers, bivariate exposure layers, and legends.

Overview

Data sources

CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis (2013–2025) provides monthly NO₂, PM₂.₅, and PM₁₀ concentration fields. ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover provides 2021 and 2023 land-cover maps from the ArcGIS Living Atlas. WorldPop provides unconstrained population counts for 2023 at 100 m resolution. FAO GAUL Level 2 administrative boundaries support district-level exposure analysis.

Processing workflow

The workflow includes CAMS NetCDF import and mesh aggregation, yearly averaging with r.series, concentration reclassification by EU thresholds, AMAC calculation (2021–2023), land-cover change detection by raster combination, binary transition masks, zonal statistics on pollutant change in transition zones, population quantile classification, bivariate exposure mapping with GAUL Level 2 districts, and dissolved pie-chart preparation.

Main results

All three pollutant AMAC maps show negative mean change over valid pixels between 2021 and 2023: NO₂ −1.765 µg/m³, PM₂.₅ −1.229 µg/m³, and PM₁₀ −1.657 µg/m³. PM₁₀ Trees-transition zones also return negative mean AMAC values for stable trees, trees gain, and trees loss.

WebGIS output

The WebGIS page displays the produced layers with at least two basemaps (OpenStreetMap and a second basemap), scale line, full-screen control, mouse position, layer controls, and map legends for pollutant change, concentration classes, land-cover transitions, and bivariate exposure classes.
